A Birthday Tribute

To Appa, with all the love I do not say every day

Dear Appa, Happy Birthday. I may not speak to you every day, and sometimes it may look as if I do not show enough care or love. But that is never because you mean less to me. I know you carry work, responsibility, and many burdens, so many times I stay silent even when my heart is full.

You are one of my greatest happinesses in life, even though I have not told you that enough. From my childhood itself, whenever I asked for something, you and Mummy tried your best to give it to me. Even when money was not there at the right time, later, when it became possible, you still bought it for me. In my life, I have asked for many things, and you have given me more than I could ever measure.

If I get angry now and then, it is not because I do not value you. My mind is under pressure from projects, studies, friends, and many thoughts I do not always know how to carry. I have never lived away from home in a hostel, so sometimes my stress stays inside the house and comes out as anger. But behind all of that, Appa, there is only love.

I have a dream for the future. After I begin earning, I do not want you to sit in the shop at Akshaya Traders every day. I want you to give it on rent and live peacefully. I do not want your property for myself. That is your property, and it is our family's dignity. I have a different mindset. I want to build my own life, and I want everything to go to Akshaya.

My dream is simple in my heart, even if it looks grand in words: I want you and Mummy to rest, sleep peacefully, watch television without worry, and live in a beautiful big home built by me. I imagine a place with open space, a farm, animals, birds, comfort, security, and every facility that makes life easy. I want technology to serve you, not tire you. I want you both to enjoy life with dignity, safety, and happiness.

More than all of that, I want you to know this: the biggest gift in my life is not money, not wealth, and not comfort. It is you and Mummy. You paid for my life from school to college and made me able to stand in this world. That is the greatest blessing I could ever receive. I am the luckiest child in the world, not because I am rich, but because I have parents like you.

I love you, Appa. Anyway, Happy Birthday Daddy 🌍🫂
